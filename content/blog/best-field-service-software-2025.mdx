## 2. Jobber - The Solid Middle Ground

**What It Does Well:**
- Strong scheduling features
- Good mobile apps
- Decent client hub
- Route optimization

**What Frustrated Me:**
- More expensive than it should be
- Some features feel half-baked
- Customer support can be slow
- Feels like they're focused on enterprise now

**Pricing:** Starts at $69/user/month

**Best For:** Established businesses ready to pay more for brand recognition

---

## 3. Housecall Pro - Heavy on Marketing

**Strengths:**
- Built-in marketing automation
- Good for customer acquisition
- Online booking works well
- Payment processing integrated

**Weaknesses:**
- EXPENSIVE at $89/user minimum
- Lots of features I never used
- Can feel overwhelming
- Contract lock-in

**Pricing:** $89-$275/user/month

**Best For:** Home service companies focused on marketing and growth

---

## 4. ServiceTitan - Enterprise Beast

**When It Makes Sense:**
- You have 50+ employees
- Need complex call center features
- Want extensive reporting
- Have dedicated admin staff

**Why I Didn't Choose It:**
- Obscenely expensive (think $15K+ per month)
- Takes weeks to implement
- Overkill for small businesses
- Need training to use it

**Pricing:** Custom (aka very expensive)

**Best For:** Large home service companies with serious budgets

## What Actually Matters in FSM Software

After using all of these, here's what really moves the needle:

### 1. **Time to Value**
Can you get it working in an hour or does it take weeks? Time is money.

### 2. **Mobile Experience**
Your techs live in the app. If it sucks on mobile, it sucks everywhere.

### 3. **Simple Invoicing**
Can you create and send an invoice in under 2 minutes? If not, you're losing money.

### 4. **Actual Support**
When something breaks, can you talk to a human or just submit a ticket?

### 5. **Fair Pricing**
Are they transparent about costs or do you need to "contact sales"?
